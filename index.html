Mancala Game Rules

Introduction

Mancala is a family of games of African origin, known as sowing games, or counting and capturing games. They are played on boards with hollows containing seeds.

Board

The board has 2 sets of aligned central hollows and are flanked by 2 stores (larger hollows). Each player controls the hollows on their side as well as the store to their right.

Pieces

The game pieces are called seeds and are placed inside the hollows.

Playing:

Beginning of the Game

At the beginning of the game the stores are empty (no seeds) and the remaining hollows have the same number of seeds, this number is entered in the configurations and by default its value is 4.

How to Play

Players sow their seeds alternately from one of their hollows. Depending on the situation of the last seed, the player can play again or keep some of the opponent's seeds. The game ends when one of the players runs out of seeds.

Start to Sow

* Choose a hollow on your side (other than the store)
* Collect all N seeds from this hollow
* Place 1 seed in the next N hollows
* Sowing is done in a counterclockwise direction.
* If the next hollow to be sown is your store, put the seed there too
* If there are seeds left, continue on the opponent's side
* If it hits the opponent's store, don't sow, and continue to sow on your side

Special Cases

Last seed in storage

If the last seed to be sown is placed in your storage, then the player plays again

Last seed in empty cavity

If the last seed to be sown is placed in an empty hollow, the player must transfer either the seed placed in the empty hollow or the seeds in the opposite hollow on the opponent's side to player's storage.

Finish

The game ends when one of the players cannot play because all their hollows are empty. The other player must collect the seeds present in their hollows and place them in their store. The player whose store has the most seeds wins.
